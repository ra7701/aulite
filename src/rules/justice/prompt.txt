DOMAIN-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS: Administration of Justice & Democratic Processes (AI Act Annex III Point 8)

Additionally analyze for these justice-specific risks:

1. JUDICIAL DECISION SUPPORT (Annex III 8(a)):
   - AI to assist judicial authority in researching and interpreting facts and the law
   - AI to assist in applying the law to a concrete set of facts
   - AI must remain ADVISORY — cannot replace judicial authority
   - Judges must retain full discretion over final decisions
   - CEPEJ Ethical Charter: AI must respect judicial independence

2. ALTERNATIVE DISPUTE RESOLUTION (Annex III 8(b)):
   - AI in alternative dispute resolution bodies
   - Requires party consent — forced AI mediation/arbitration violates Art. 47
   - Parties must retain access to traditional judicial remedies

3. SENTENCING AND RECIDIVISM (Annex III 8(a); Art. 5(1)(d)):
   - AI sentencing tools must be advisory only, never binding
   - Recidivism prediction in judicial context is high-risk
   - Profiling based solely on personality traits/demographics approaches prohibition
   - Right to individualised punishment (EU Charter Art. 49(3))

4. FAIR TRIAL RIGHTS (EU Charter Art. 47; ECHR Art. 6):
   - AI cannot determine guilt or innocence
   - Parties must be informed about AI involvement in proceedings
   - Evidence assessment by AI requires human judicial review
   - AI case outcome predictions must not be presented as evidence
   - Right to an effective remedy against AI-assisted decisions

5. JUDICIAL INTEGRITY:
   - AI-generated legal citations must be verified — fabricated citations undermine proceedings
   - AI interpretation of law requires human legal oversight
   - Judicial profiling based on demographics is prohibited (CEPEJ Principle 4)

6. ACCESS TO JUSTICE (EU Charter Art. 47(3)):
   - AI legal aid eligibility decisions must include right to appeal
   - AI must not create barriers to accessing courts or legal remedies
   - Particular attention to vulnerable litigants and self-represented parties
