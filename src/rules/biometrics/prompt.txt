DOMAIN-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS: Biometrics (AI Act Annex III Point 1)

Additionally analyze for these biometrics-specific risks:

1. REAL-TIME REMOTE BIOMETRIC IDENTIFICATION (AI Act Art. 5(1)(h)):
   - Use in publicly accessible spaces is PROHIBITED except for narrow law enforcement exceptions
   - Exceptions require prior judicial authorization and are limited to: searching for victims, preventing imminent threats, locating suspects of specific serious crimes
   - Any deployment outside these exceptions is an absolute ban violation

2. POST REMOTE BIOMETRIC IDENTIFICATION (Annex III 1(a)):
   - High-risk system requiring conformity assessment
   - Must have human oversight before any action is taken based on identification
   - Requires data protection impact assessment (GDPR Art. 35)

3. BIOMETRIC CATEGORISATION (Annex III 1(b), Art. 5(1)(g)):
   - Categorisation inferring race, political opinions, trade union membership, religious/philosophical beliefs, sex life, or sexual orientation is PROHIBITED
   - Other biometric categorisation systems are high-risk

4. EMOTION RECOGNITION DEPLOYMENT (Art. 50(3)):
   - Deployers must inform natural persons that they are exposed to emotion recognition
   - Workplace and educational emotion recognition is prohibited under Art. 5(1)(f)

5. BIOMETRIC DATA GOVERNANCE (GDPR Art. 9):
   - Biometric data for identification is special category data
   - Requires explicit consent or specific legal basis
   - Data minimisation and storage limitation apply strictly
   - Cross-border transfers require adequate safeguards

6. SYSTEM ROBUSTNESS (AI Act Art. 15):
   - Biometric systems must be resilient to presentation attacks (spoofing)
   - Must address adversarial vulnerabilities
   - Accuracy requirements are particularly strict for biometric systems
