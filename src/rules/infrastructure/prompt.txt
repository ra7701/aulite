DOMAIN-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS: Critical Infrastructure (AI Act Annex III Point 2)

Additionally analyze for these critical infrastructure-specific risks:

1. SAFETY COMPONENTS (Annex III 2(a)):
   - AI systems intended as safety components in management/operation of: road traffic, water supply, gas supply, heating supply, electricity supply
   - Must have established risk management system (Art. 9)
   - Requires conformity assessment before deployment (Art. 43)
   - Must maintain technical documentation (Art. 11)

2. DIGITAL INFRASTRUCTURE (Annex III 2(b)):
   - AI systems as safety components in digital infrastructure management
   - Road traffic management and safety systems
   - Subject to NIS2 Directive cybersecurity requirements

3. HUMAN OVERSIGHT (AI Act Art. 14):
   - Critical infrastructure AI must have human oversight mechanisms
   - Operators must be able to override AI decisions
   - Emergency responses must include human escalation paths
   - No autonomous control without human fallback

4. ROBUSTNESS AND CYBERSECURITY (AI Act Art. 15):
   - Systems must be resilient to errors, faults, and attacks
   - Must have fail-safe mechanisms and redundancy
   - Cybersecurity measures per NIS2 Directive 2022/2555
   - Must address adversarial vulnerabilities

5. RISK MANAGEMENT (AI Act Art. 9):
   - Continuous risk identification and mitigation throughout lifecycle
   - Must include foreseeable misuse scenarios
   - Residual risk must be communicated to deployers
   - Testing procedures for safety-critical applications

6. INCIDENT REPORTING (AI Act Art. 62; NIS2):
   - Serious incidents must be reported to market surveillance authorities
   - NIS2 requires notification of significant cybersecurity incidents
   - Must maintain incident logs and post-incident analysis
